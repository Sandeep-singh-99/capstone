# MediGuide AI Clinical Knowledge Reference

This document contains trusted, educational clinical information regarding standard lab tests, common medical symptoms, and specialist consultations.

---

## Standard Laboratory Parameters

### Complete Blood Count (CBC)
*   **White Blood Cells (WBC)**:
    *   *Reference Range*: 4,500 to 11,000 cells/mcL.
    *   *High Levels (Leukocytosis)*: Often indicates the body is fighting an infection, experiencing physical stress, or inflammation.
    *   *Low Levels (Leukopenia)*: May point to viral infections, autoimmune conditions, or bone marrow issues.
*   **Red Blood Cells (RBC)**:
    *   *Reference Range*: 4.3 to 5.9 million cells/mcL (Men), 3.5 to 5.5 million cells/mcL (Women).
    *   *Low Levels*: Often indicates anemia, causing fatigue and pale skin.
*   **Hemoglobin (Hgb)**:
    *   *Reference Range*: 13.5 to 17.5 g/dL (Men), 12.0 to 15.5 g/dL (Women).
    *   *Low Levels*: Suggests iron deficiency anemia, vitamin deficiencies, or chronic bleeding.
*   **Platelets (PLT)**:
    *   *Reference Range*: 150,000 to 450,000 cells/mcL.
    *   *Low Levels (Thrombocytopenia)*: Increases the risk of bleeding and bruising.

### Metabolic Panel and Lipid Panel
*   **Fasting Blood Glucose**:
    *   *Normal*: 70–99 mg/dL.
    *   *Prediabetes*: 100–125 mg/dL.
    *   *Diabetes*: 126 mg/dL or higher on multiple tests.
*   **Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c)**:
    *   *Normal*: Under 5.7%.
    *   *Prediabetes*: 5.7% to 6.4%.
    *   *Diabetes*: 6.5% or higher. Measures average blood sugar over the last 3 months.
*   **Total Cholesterol**:
    *   *Desirable*: Under 200 mg/dL.
    *   *High*: 240 mg/dL or higher. Elevated levels increase cardiovascular risk.
*   **Low-Density Lipoprotein (LDL)**:
    *   *Optimal*: Under 100 mg/dL. Often called "bad" cholesterol.
*   **High-Density Lipoprotein (HDL)**:
    *   *Optimal*: 60 mg/dL or higher. Often called "good" cholesterol because it helps clear other forms of cholesterol.

## Symptom to Specialist Mapping Guide

*   **Chest Pain / Palpitations**:
    *   *Recommended Specialist*: Cardiologist (Heart specialist).
    *   *Educational context*: Chest pain can indicate cardiovascular issues such as angina or arrhythmias, but also muscle strain or acid reflux. Seek emergency medical attention for severe, crushing chest pain.
*   **Skin Rash / Itching / Mole Changes**:
    *   *Recommended Specialist*: Dermatologist (Skin specialist).
    *   *Educational context*: Rashes can stem from allergic contact dermatitis, eczema, psoriasis, or infections. Changing moles should be evaluated for skin cancer.
*   **Eye Pain / Blurry Vision / Double Vision**:
    *   *Recommended Specialist*: Ophthalmologist (Eye MD).
    *   *Educational context*: Blurry vision can be caused by refractive errors, cataracts, glaucoma, or dry eye syndrome.
*   **Severe Headaches / Migraine / Numbness / Tingling**:
    *   *Recommended Specialist*: Neurologist (Nervous system specialist).
    *   *Educational context*: Tingling and numbness may stem from nerve compression (like carpal tunnel) or peripheral neuropathy. Severe, sudden-onset headaches ("thunderclap") require immediate emergency care.
*   **Joint Pain / Bone Fracture / Swollen Joints**:
    *   *Recommended Specialist*: Orthopedist (Musculoskeletal specialist).
    *   *Educational context*: Joint pain is commonly related to osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, gout, or ligament sprains.
*   **Abdominal Pain / Persistent Nausea / Acid Reflux**:
    *   *Recommended Specialist*: Gastroenterologist (Digestive specialist).
    *   *Educational context*: Chronic heartburn or abdominal pain can be associated with GERD, peptic ulcers, or irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).
*   **Chronic Fatigue / Rapid Weight Changes / Excessive Thirst**:
    *   *Recommended Specialist*: Endocrinologist (Hormone specialist).
    *   *Educational context*: These symptoms often correlate with thyroid dysfunction (hypothyroidism/hyperthyroidism) or diabetes.
*   **Fever / Sore Throat / Cough / Cold / General Malaise**:
    *   *Recommended Specialist*: General Practitioner (Primary Care Physician).
    *   *Educational context*: Standard viral or bacterial upper respiratory infections.
